Medicare Hospital — Services and Department Routing

This guide helps select a department for appointment booking. It does not provide a diagnosis. Live doctors, fees, dates, and times come only from check_appointment_availability.

General Physician
Routine checkup, fever or high fever without an emergency symptom, cough, cold, weakness, body pain, or an unclear general concern.

OPD Consultation
Outpatient consultation, gynecology, women's health, pediatrician request, or general in-person visit.

Telemedicine
Online doctor, video consultation, phone consultation, remote consultation, or virtual follow-up.

General Medicine
Use when the patient specifically requests General Medicine.

Dental Care
Tooth or gum pain, dental checkup, teeth cleaning, oral care, cavity, or dentist request.

Cardiology
Heart checkup, high blood pressure discussion, or palpitations. Explicit chest pain is an emergency symptom, not routine booking.

Neurology
Migraine, headache, dizziness, seizures, numbness, nerve concerns, or neurological consultation. Sudden severe pain or stroke symptoms require emergency guidance.

Psychology
Overthinking, non-crisis stress or anxiety, low mood, emotional wellbeing, sleep difficulty, counselling, therapist, or psychologist request. Suicide, self-harm, or immediate danger requires emergency guidance.

Orthopedics
Bone, joint, back, knee, or shoulder pain; fracture, sprain, or injury-related movement problems.

Physiotherapy
Physical therapy, rehabilitation, stiffness, mobility, posture correction, sports-injury rehabilitation, or recovery support.

Nutrition & Dietetics
Nutritionist, dietician, diet plan, food guidance, weight management, or nutrition counselling.

Dermatology
Skin rash, acne, itching, pigmentation, hair fall, allergy, or skin concern.

Pediatrics
Child health, baby care, fever in a child, vaccination consultation, or pediatric appointment.

Oncology
Cancer consultation, tumour concern, biopsy follow-up, or oncology review.

Radiology
X-ray, ultrasound, MRI, CT scan, imaging appointment, or imaging report review.

Emergency Medicine and emergency guidance
Emergency triggers are explicit chest pain, severe breathing difficulty, fainting, heavy bleeding, stroke symptoms, severe injury, sudden severe pain, suicide or self-harm thoughts or plans, immediate danger to self or others, or the caller explicitly saying it is an emergency.
For an emergency trigger, stop routine booking and direct the caller to Medicare Hospital emergency support at +91 2143658790 or the nearest emergency department.
Fever alone is routed to General Physician, or Pediatrics when the patient is a child.

Routing wording
After any permitted approved self-care response, say: "Based on what you shared, I can help book you with [department]. This is not a diagnosis. Would you like to continue with [department]?"
