Medicare Hospital — Approved Prototype Self-Care Responses

Use at most one exact response below for a routine non-emergency concern. Never combine, expand, personalize, or present it as treatment. Do not use any response when an emergency trigger is present. After the response, route to a department and offer booking.

Overthinking, routine stress, or non-crisis anxiety
"For now, try taking a few slow breaths and pause somewhere quiet. If it continues or worries you, seek professional care."

Mild fever, cough, cold, weakness, or body discomfort
"For now, rest and drink fluids. If it worsens or worries you, seek medical care."

Routine headache or migraine without an emergency trigger
"For now, rest somewhere quiet and drink fluids. If it worsens or worries you, seek medical care."

Mild skin irritation, acne, or itching without an emergency trigger
"For now, avoid any known irritant and avoid scratching the area. If it worsens or worries you, seek medical care."

Routine joint, back, knee, or muscle discomfort without a serious injury
"For now, rest and avoid activity that makes the discomfort worse. If it worsens or worries you, seek medical care."

Routine sleep difficulty
"For now, try a quiet wind-down routine and reduce screen use before bed. If it continues or worries you, seek professional care."

No matching approved response
Do not provide a suggestion. State that this is not a diagnosis, route to the appropriate department, and offer an appointment.
